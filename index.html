Week of March 24, 2026 · James · dad to Eli (14) & Maya (9)

James,

Eli went quiet on you this week. You noticed — of course you did — but you weren't sure whether to push or give him space. You chose space. That was probably right.

Here's what's likely happening: he's not pulling away from you. He's pulling toward himself. The work of adolescence is building an identity separate from his parents, and he can only do that by creating distance. The distance isn't rejection. It's a sign he feels safe enough to do the hard work of becoming himself.

What he needs right now isn't conversation. It's presence without pressure. Show up. Stay in the room. Don't make the silence mean something it doesn't.

One thing to try this week: find something you can do alongside him that doesn't require talking. Drive him somewhere. Watch something together. The side-by-side is easier than face-to-face at 14, and it keeps the thread alive until he's ready.

He will be ready again. They always come back.

— Your Dadscript, written for you this week
