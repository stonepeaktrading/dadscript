This week · Marcus · dad to Amara (8) & Jaylen (14)

Marcus,

Something happened this week that you almost didn't notice.

Amara was doing her homework at the kitchen table. You walked past, glanced at her work, said "that's good" — and kept moving. She watched you walk away. Then she looked back down and smiled to herself.

She wasn't smiling because you praised her work. She was smiling because you saw her. That's what 8 looks like — she's testing something quiet. Whether you notice her when nothing is wrong.

With Jaylen it's different. He's started making jokes and then watching your face before he laughs. He's not looking for approval. He's checking whether the person he's becoming is someone you actually want to know.

Laugh first sometimes. Before he does. Let him see that he surprised you.

The goal isn't to fix things. It's to make the good moments land. Your kids will remember the ordinary Tuesdays more than the big occasions. They'll remember that you noticed.

This week — one moment with each of them. Not planned. Just noticed.

— Your Dadscript, written for you this week

Why this exists

Built by a dad who needed it first.

My daughter is 8. She lives 5 hours away. When I do get time with her I used to default to just keeping her entertained. Movies. Food. Activities. But entertained isn't the same as present. And I knew it.

Over 10 years in business I've met hundreds of fathers. The same questions keep coming up — what does my 9-year-old need that my 5-year-old didn't? My teenager has gone silent, do I push or give space? I only see my kids on weekends, how do I make those days count?

I started writing letters to myself. To help me show up better on the days I do have her. Other dads found out and asked me to write for them too. One told me it was the first time someone described exactly what he was feeling without him having to explain it.

That's what Dadscript is. And the 500 founding dads who join now will shape everything it becomes.
